Calla Lily (Zantedeschia aethiopica) Care Guide

Environment

Light

  • Bright, Indirect Light: Calla Lilies thrive in bright, indirect light. They can tolerate some direct sunlight, especially in the morning or late afternoon, but avoid harsh midday sun.

Soil

  • Rich, Well-Draining Soil: Use a high-quality potting mix rich in organic matter. A mix designed for flowering plants or a blend of peat, compost, and sand works well.

Temperature

  • Moderate Temperatures: Calla Lilies prefer temperatures between 60-75°F (15-24°C). They can tolerate slightly cooler temperatures but should be protected from frost.

Routine jobs

Watering

  • Consistent Moisture: Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Water regularly during the growing season (spring and summer) and reduce watering in the dormant period (fall and winter).
  • Avoid Overwatering: Ensure proper drainage to prevent root rot. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out slightly between waterings.

Feeding

  • Regular Fertilization: Feed Calla Lilies every 2-3 weeks during the growing season with a balanced liquid fertilizer. Reduce feeding during the dormant period.

Potting

  • Container Choice: Choose a pot with drainage holes. Calla Lilies can be grown in containers or in the ground if the soil drains well.
  • Repotting: Repot as needed, usually every 1-2 years, in fresh soil to ensure continued growth and flowering.

Troubleshooting

  • Common Pests: Watch for aphids, spider mites, and whiteflies. Treat infestations with insecticidal soap or neem oil.
  • Leaf Care: Remove any yellowing or damaged leaves to maintain plant health and appearance.

Flowering

  • Bloom Period: Calla Lilies typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Proper care and fertilization can encourage more blooms.
  • Post-Bloom Care: After flowering, reduce watering and allow the plant to enter a dormant period. Resume regular care when new growth appears.
